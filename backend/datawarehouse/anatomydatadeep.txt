The trachea splits into the left and right Primary Bronchi, which enter the lungs and branch into secondary and tertiary bronchi, then into tiny Bronchioles. These end in clusters of Alveoli, microscopic air sacs where gas exchange occurs. Oxygen diffuses across the thin alveolar membrane into the blood-filled capillaries, while carbon dioxide diffuses from the blood into the alveoli to be exhaled. This process is driven by partial pressure gradients. The lungs are encased in a double-layered serous membrane called the Pleura. The pleural cavity contains fluid that reduces friction during breathing. The Diaphragm, a large sheet of muscle at the base of the chest, is the primary driver of inhalation.
